Title: Team Collaboration Network Graph

Prompt: Need a collaboration network showing 8 team members as circles connected by lines with varying connection counts. Make circle sizes match their connection counts - more connections mean bigger circles. Group connected people closer together and keep circles from overlapping. When I click someone's circle, animate their connections with pulsing blue lines flowing from the center to the edges and dim everyone else with a fade-out animation. Draw thin dotted connector lines from each highlighted connection to a sidebar positioned directly below the network graph showing the person's name, connection count, and collaborators. Display overall network density panel exactly below the sidebar. Clicking again deselects with a reverse fade-in animation. Load with sample team data showing realistic collaboration patterns.

Required libraries: react, tailwindcss, lucide-react

## Justification

The Team Collaboration Network Graph achieved 43% pass rate (9/21 criteria) with cascading failures from complex animations and precise positioning requirements. The visualization displays eight circles labeled with team member names (C1, C2 passed) and draws all connection lines accurately (C3 passed). Circle sizing worked correctly with three distinct sizes (C4 passed). However, the model failed C5 by placing members in a random scattered layout instead of positioning connected members closer together - Alice, Bob, Carol, and Dan should form a tight cluster but were spread across opposite corners. The layout algorithm failed C6 with Bob's circle overlapping partially with Eve's circle, which caused C7 to fail automatically - without proper non-overlapping positioning, the line routing algorithm couldn't avoid crossing through circles. Click interactions worked: clicking a circle highlighted it (C8 passed) and applied blue highlighting to connections (C9 passed), but C10 failed catastrophically - the model showed static blue lines instead of animating them with pulsing effects flowing from center to edges. The model failed C11 by completely hiding non-connected members instead of applying fade-out animation to dim them. The model failed C12 by drawing no dotted connector lines from highlighted connections to the sidebar, breaking visual correspondence. The sidebar appeared but failed C13 - it was positioned to the right of the network graph instead of directly below it, causing C17 to fail in cascade since the density panel couldn't be "exactly below the sidebar" when the sidebar wasn't below the network. The sidebar showed the person's name (C14 passed), connection count (C15 passed), and list of connected names (C16 passed). Network density calculation worked (C18 passed) and the density panel displayed the value (C19 passed). The deselect interaction worked (C20 passed) but failed C21 by showing an instant state change instead of applying reverse fade-in animation when deselecting.
