Category: Data_Visualization

Prompt style: Conversational

Title: Customer Journey Chord Diagram

Prompt: I want to build a customer journey visualization showing how users move between five touchpoints using a chord diagram with 3D curved ribbons that have depth shading. Arrange touchpoints around a circle with ribbons connecting them - ribbon widths match user flow volumes. Flows are directional. When I click a touchpoint, animate the connected ribbons with flowing particles from source to destination and update a stats panel positioned directly below the diagram showing total users coming in, going out, and the net difference - color that green with a glow effect if positive, red with glow if negative. Draw thin dotted lines from each filtered ribbon to the stats panel. Position a legend exactly below the stats panel explaining touchpoint meanings. Small flows get grouped as "Low Traffic". Preload with sample customer journey data.

Required libraries: react, tailwindcss, lucide-react

## Justification

The Customer Journey Chord Diagram achieved 41% pass rate (9/22 criteria) with cascading failures from 3D effects, animations, and precise positioning. The visualization displays five touchpoint arcs evenly spaced around a circle (C1 passed) labeled Website, Email, Social Media, Store, and Phone Support (C2 passed) with curved ribbons connecting them (C3 passed). However, C4 failed catastrophically - the model rendered ribbons as flat 2D curves instead of applying 3D depth shading, losing the visual tube effect. The ribbon width calculation failed C5 - instead of being proportional to flow volumes, all ribbons appeared roughly equal thickness. The model completely failed C6 by treating flows as bidirectional - it drew a single ribbon between Website and Email representing the sum of both directions (160 users) instead of two separate directional ribbons for Website→Email (120) and Email→Website (40), which caused C15 and C16 to fail automatically since inbound/outbound calculations require directional flows. The grouping logic failed C7 and C8 - flows under 50 users were drawn as individual solid ribbons instead of being grouped into a single dashed "Low Traffic" ribbon. Ribbon positioning worked correctly, avoiding touchpoint arc overlaps (C9 passed). Click interactions functioned: clicking a touchpoint filtered to show only connected ribbons (C10 passed), but C11 failed - filtered ribbons displayed as static curves instead of animating with flowing particles from source to destination. The model failed C12 by drawing no dotted lines from filtered ribbons to the stats panel, breaking visual correspondence. The stats panel appeared but failed C13 - it was positioned to the right of the diagram instead of directly below it, causing C21 to fail in cascade since the legend couldn't be "exactly below the stats panel" when the panel wasn't below the diagram. The touchpoint name displayed correctly (C14 passed). Inbound/outbound calculations failed C15 and C16 due to bidirectionality error. Flow totals displayed in the panel (C17 passed). Net flow calculation failed C18, color coding failed C19, and glow effect failed C20 - the net flow displayed without any glow effect. The legend appeared (C22 passed).
