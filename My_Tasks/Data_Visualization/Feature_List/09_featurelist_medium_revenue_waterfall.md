Title: Revenue Waterfall Chart

Prompt: Build a revenue waterfall chart visualizing monthly financial changes with an opening balance and several positive and negative changes leading to an ending balance. Display each change as a vertical bar with 3D depth shading positioned to show the running balance - bars for increases extend upward from the previous balance, bars for decreases extend downward. Color positive changes green with animated rising particles, negative red with falling particles, opening/ending blue. Draw curved connector lines between bars. Display amounts inside bars. Draw thin dotted lines from the largest increase and decrease bars to a summary panel positioned directly below the chart showing net change color-coded green or red, largest increase, and largest decrease. Position a legend exactly below the summary panel explaining bar colors. Preload with sample monthly revenue data.

Required libraries: react, tailwindcss, lucide-react, recharts

## Justification

The Revenue Waterfall Chart achieved 43% pass rate (10/23 criteria) with cascading failures from 3D effects, animations, and positioning. The chart displays an opening balance bar from zero to $100K (C1 passed), six monthly change bars (C2 passed), and an ending balance bar from zero to the final amount (C3 passed). However, C4 failed catastrophically - the model rendered all bars as flat 2D rectangles instead of applying 3D depth shading, losing the solid column effect. The floating bar positioning completely failed C5 and C6 - instead of floating at the previous month's ending value, all bars started at zero like a standard bar chart. February's +$25K bar should have floated from $100K to $125K, but instead started at zero and reached $25K, destroying the waterfall flow visualization, which caused C7 to fail automatically since curved connector lines between floating bars require correct floating positions - the model drew straight lines between bar tops instead of curves. The color coding worked: positive changes appeared green (C8 passed), but C9 failed - green bars displayed as solid color without rising particle animation. Negative changes appeared red (C10 passed), but C11 failed - red bars displayed without falling particle animation. Opening/ending balances appeared blue (C12 passed). Dollar amounts displayed centered inside each bar (C13 passed). The extreme value identification worked correctly (C14, C15 passed): April +$40K was the largest increase, March -$15K was the largest decrease. However, the model failed C16 by drawing no dotted lines from these extreme bars to the summary panel. The summary panel appeared but failed C17 - it was positioned to the right of the chart instead of directly below it, causing C22 to fail in cascade since the legend couldn't be "exactly below the summary panel" when the panel wasn't below the chart. Net change calculation worked correctly (C18 passed), showing "Net Change: +$60K" with proper formatting (C19 passed) and green coloring (C20 passed). The summary panel displayed largest increase and decrease correctly (C21 passed). The legend appeared (C23 passed).
