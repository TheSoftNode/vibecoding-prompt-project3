Title: Employee Performance Parallel Coordinates

Prompt: Build a parallel coordinates chart comparing employees across four metrics: Quality Score, Speed Score, Customer Rating, and Projects Completed. Display four vertical axes with 3D depth effects evenly spaced. Draw each employee as a line connecting their values across axes with animated flowing particles along the lines. Color and style lines based on Quality Score zones - high scores get blue thick lines with fast particles, low scores get red thin lines with slow particles, middle range gray medium lines. Provide range sliders to filter by Quality and Projects - show only employees matching both ranges and update visible count. Draw thin dotted lines from filtered employee lines to a summary panel positioned directly below the chart displaying metric averages from visible employees only, or show "No employees match" when filtered out. Position a legend exactly below the summary panel explaining line colors and thickness. Preload with sample employee data.

Required libraries: react, tailwindcss, lucide-react

## Justification

The Employee Performance Parallel Coordinates chart achieved 42% pass rate (10/24 criteria) with cascading failures from 3D effects, animations, and positioning. The visualization displays four vertical axes evenly spaced (C1 passed), but failed C2 catastrophically by rendering all axes as flat 2D lines instead of applying 3D depth effects, losing the solid column effect. Axes labeled correctly (C3 passed) with scales 0-100, 0-100, 0-100, and 0-20 (C4 passed). However, the star rating conversion completely failed C5 - instead of converting ratings to the 0-100 scale (where Alice's 4.5 stars should plot at 90), the model plotted ratings directly on a 1-5 range, causing all customer rating values to cluster in the bottom 5% of the axis. Eight lines correctly connected employee values across axes (C6 passed), but C7 failed catastrophically - lines displayed as static paths instead of animating with flowing particles along paths, which caused C10 and C12 to fail automatically since fast/slow particle animation requires particles to exist in the first place. The zone-based styling calculation worked (C8 passed) identifying quality zones. Lines appeared blue for high quality (C9 passed), but failed to show thick stroke - all lines appeared the same thickness. Lines appeared red for low quality (C11 passed), gray for middle-tier (C13 passed). The filter sliders displayed (C14 passed), but the filtering logic failed C15 critically - when setting Quality min=75 and Projects min=12, the chart showed Bob (Quality 60, Projects 18) using OR logic instead of AND logic. The hide behavior worked correctly (C16 passed). The model failed C17 by drawing no dotted lines from filtered employee lines to the summary panel. The summary panel appeared but failed C18 - it was positioned to the right of the chart instead of directly below it, causing C23 to fail in cascade since the legend couldn't be "exactly below the summary panel" when the panel wasn't below the chart. The employee count showed (C19 passed) but with incorrect value due to C15 failure. The average calculation failed C20 by continuing to include all 8 employees instead of recalculating from only visible ones. The summary panel displayed averages (C21 passed) but they were incorrect. The "No employees match" message (C22) was untested. Legend appeared (C24 passed).
