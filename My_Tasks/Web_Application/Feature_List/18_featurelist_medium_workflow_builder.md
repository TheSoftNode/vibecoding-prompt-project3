Title: Workflow Builder with Step Dependencies

Prompt: Build a workflow builder with three connected panels showing step visualization. Display a templates panel positioned at top-left showing three template options: "Basic Flow", "Advanced Flow", and "Custom Flow". Display a steps list panel positioned at bottom-left directly below the templates panel showing three pre-populated steps: "Initialize" step, "Execute" step, and "Complete" step. Display a diagram canvas panel positioned at top-right showing the visual diagram. Display an "Add Step" button and a "Delete Step" button below the steps list. Load initial template options and pre-populated steps on startup. Thin dotted connecting lines connect each step in the bottom-left list to its corresponding node in the top-right canvas. When users click a template option, load that template's workflow steps into the steps list and update the canvas diagram to show the new steps. When users click a step in the list, highlight both the list item and its canvas node, and change the connecting line from dotted to solid. When users click the Add Step button, add a new step labeled "New Step" to the bottom of the steps list and add its corresponding node to the canvas. When users click the Delete Step button, remove the currently highlighted step from both the steps list and the canvas diagram. Initialize appears at the top of the canvas, Execute in the middle, and Complete at the bottom.

Required libraries: react, tailwindcss, lucide-react, react-beautiful-dnd

## Justification

The Workflow Builder with Step Dependencies will be evaluated based on its ability to coordinate three interconnected panels with template switching, step management, visual linking, and canvas positioning. Success requires displaying the templates panel at top-left showing three template options (Basic Flow, Advanced Flow, Custom Flow), the steps list panel at bottom-left positioned directly below the templates panel showing three pre-populated steps (Initialize, Execute, Complete) with Add Step and Delete Step buttons below, and the diagram canvas panel at top-right. Thin dotted connecting lines must connect each step in the bottom-left list to its corresponding node in the top-right canvas. When users click a template option, the implementation must load that template's workflow steps into the steps list and update the canvas diagram. When users click a step in the list, the implementation must highlight both the list item and its canvas node while changing the connecting line from dotted to solid style. When users click the Add Step button, a new step labeled "New Step" must be added to the bottom of the steps list and canvas. When users click the Delete Step button, the highlighted step must be removed from both the list and canvas. The canvas must position Initialize at the top, Execute in the middle, and Complete at the bottom. This task tests multi-panel layout positioning with specific spatial relationships, template loading interaction, step addition/deletion functionality, visual linking through connecting lines with dynamic style changes, and canvas positioning hierarchy.
