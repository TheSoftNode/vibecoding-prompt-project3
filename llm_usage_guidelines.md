LLM (Large Language Model) Usage Policy
AI tools should be used carefully. LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) can help with minor drafting and research, but cannot replace human judgment or compromise task integrity.

✅ Allowed (With Your Oversight)
Ideas & Brainstorming: Generating task concepts, exploring prompt variations, and suggesting rubric criteria angles.
Prompt Review: Checking tone consistency and grammar.
Rubric Review: Identifying vague language, spotting compound criteria, and suggesting atomicity improvements.
Grammar & Clarity: Polishing criterion descriptions, rationale text, and prompt wording.
🔍 Required Human Verification
You are responsible for confirming quality by manually reviewing all LLM outputs:
Verify criteria are truly atomic (one single thing to check).
Confirm no forbidden words (e.g., correctly, properly, appropriately).
Check completeness against prompt requirements.
Validate that criteria are verifiable from the screenshot or code.
🛠️ Recommended Tools
Grammarly (or similar) for final grammar and spelling checks.
🚫 Prohibited
Core deliverables must not be generated by LLMs:
Rubric content
Prompt content
📝 Requirements
Attribution: If an LLM helped with non-core drafting (e.g., wording), you remain fully accountable for the final result.
Originality: Keep your authentic voice; avoid "model-ish" or robotic language.
